Residence Counselling Services

Overview of the Service

  • Western’s Residence Counselling services aim to offer free, non-crisis video call services for students living in residence.
  • Students can email needtotalk@uwo.ca to book an appointment.
  • Website: Click here

What kinds of services do they offer?

  • Short-term mental health support for students in residence, provided by registered social workers.
  • Emphasis is placed on resolving interpersonal issues (i.e. with roommates or significant others), dealing with homesickness, and other issues commonly faced by students living away from home.
  • Can direct students to other support services on campus, such as Learning Development and Success or Accessible Education, as necessary

What kinds of services do they offer?

  • What happens when you email?
  • Email needtotalk@uwo.ca using your Western email account.
  • You will confirm a time to speak on Zoom via an email exchange.

Email template:

Hello,

I hope this email finds you well. My name is [your name], my student number is [your student number] and I am currently living in [residence name] Hall. I am reaching out to book an appointment with Residence Counselling services, and I am available [list of times that you are available in the upcoming week]. Please let me know if any of these time slots are available, or if there are alternative time slots that are free.

Thank you!
[Your name]

Strategies for meetings

  • Don’t be shy / embarrassed to discuss your struggles: the counsellors are experienced with providing help for a variety of issues, but they need to understand what you’re going through first

What happens afterwards?

  • You can book a follow-up appointment if you feel that it would be beneficial.
  • If you would like to meet with a specific counsellor, you can request them by name.
