Western’s Mental Health Resource Guide

Presented by Western’s Jack.org Chapter

Hi there! Thank you for accessing this guide - we hope it can help you access some of the mental health and wellness resources available to you so that you can get the help you need, whatever that may be.

Contained in this guide are links to information regarding 3 services at Western, and links to 7 helplines, not restricted to Western students.

If you have comments or feedback you want to provide us on this guide, please feel free to let us know here. The answers are completely anonymous and confidential and the feedback will only be used to improve the quality and inclusivity of this guide, so that it can better support students.

Western-Specific Resources

For each resource, we have linked a document that provides the following information in more detail:
  • What services they offer
  • How to book an appointment + who to call/email and what happens when you do this
  • Strategies for your appointments/meetings, email templates and more.
  • What happens after your meeting/appointment

Community Resources

RESOURCE 4: Good2Talk
About this service:

  • FREE, available 24/7/365, confidential and anonymous support
  • Support students through a wide range of issues that may be impacting mental health and well-being
  • Non-crisis support
  • Offered in English and French
How can I access this service?
  • Call: 1-866-925-5454 (non-crisis)
  • You will connected to a professional counselling who can provide information and referrals about services and supports for mental health on and off-campus
Who can use this service?
  • Individuals who attend a post-secondary institution or Indigenous Institute in Ontario

RESOURCE 5: Crisis Text Line
About this service:

  • FREE, available 24/7/365, confidential and anonymous support
  • Support students through a wide range of issues that may be impacting mental health and well-being
  • Offered in English and French
How can I access this service?
  • Crisis Text Line: Text “GOOD2TALKON” to 686868
  • You will be connected to a trained volunteer Crisis Responder
Who can use this service?
  • Individuals who attend a post-secondary institution or Indigenous Institute in Ontario
